## SLIDE 2: THEORETICAL BACKGROUND

### Title: Theoretical Background

**Four Economic Theories:**

| Theory | Mechanism | Key Citation | Hsinchu Application |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Human Capital** ‚úÖ | Education ‚Üí Skills ‚Üí Income | Becker (1964) | Higher education areas = Higher income |
| **Agglomeration** | Clustering ‚Üí Knowledge spillovers ‚Üí Wage premium | Krugman (1991) | Proximity to Science Park = Higher income |
| **Demographics** | Aging ‚Üí Lower labor force ‚Üí Reduced income | Lee & Mason (2010) | High aging areas = Lower income |
| **Environmental Disamenity** | Noise ‚Üí Residential sorting ‚Üí Income stratification | Nelson (2004) | Airbase proximity = Lower income |

**Empirical Support:**
- Human Capital: 60+ years evidence, 10% wage return per education year
- Agglomeration: 30-40% tech cluster wage premium
- Aging: 10% increase in 60+ ‚Üí 5.5% GDP decrease
- Disamenity: 5-15% property value reduction near airports
